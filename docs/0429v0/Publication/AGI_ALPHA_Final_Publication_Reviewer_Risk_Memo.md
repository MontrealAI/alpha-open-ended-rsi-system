# Reviewer-risk memo

This memo identifies likely external-review objections and the paper's defenses.

1. **Too broad?** The paper is intentionally comprehensive; the Reader's Map, At-a-Glance table, and canonical architecture figure create a navigable spine.
2. **Core contribution?** A proof-bearing, validator-gated, RSI-governed substrate for intelligence organizations.
3. **Beyond multi-agent systems?** The paper adds proof-settlement, namespace authority, evidence dockets, alpha-WU metrology, value-to-energy reinvestment, and deterministic RSI governance.
4. **Beyond marketplaces?** It treats invention-space as labor-space through MandateEpochs, ProofBundles, QD archives, causal substrate, and validator-gated promotion.
5. **Beyond AI-GAs/open-endedness?** It adds proof gates, settlement, namespace authority, ECI, drift sentinel, and Evidence Dockets.
6. **Beyond safety governance?** It integrates safety as outcome authority: risk, evidence, baseline, persistence, replay, and validator gates.
7. **Actually implemented?** MontrealAI repositories provide implementation scaffolds, demos, contracts, CI, nodes, docs, local/devnet proof surfaces, and runbooks.
8. **Empirically proven?** Not yet as SOTA; comparative real-task Evidence Dockets remain the burden.
9. **Metrics measurable?** Metrics are labeled empirical, proxy, or strategic. Promotion rules specify measurement.
10. **Falsification?** Failure against baselines under equal budgets, unreplayable traces, unsafe promotion, high false acceptance, or poor cost/risk performance falsifies operational claims.
